1.Phibsoo wildlife sanctuary

Located in the southern part of Bhutan, Phibsoo is one of the smallest wildlife sanctuaries but is rich in biodiversity. It is known for its tropical and subtropical forests and is home to species like the Bengal tiger, clouded leopard, gulden langur, and various species of deer.

2. Sakteng Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated in the eastern part of Bhutan, this sanctuary is famous for its unique biodiversity and the protection of the rare and elusive red panda. It is also home to a range of birds, including the Himalayan monal, as well as various mammals and plant species.

3. Jigme Dorji National Park

While technically a national park, Jigme Dorji is one of Bhutan's most important protected areas and serves a similar function to a wildlife sanctuary. Located in the western part of the country, it houses a diverse range of wildlife, including the endangered snow leopard, takin, Himalayan black bear, and various species of birds and flora.

4.Jigme Singye Wangchuck National Park

Another national park in Bhutan, Jigme Singye Wangchuck also acts as a sanctuary for a wide variety of wildlife. This park is located in the central part of Bhutan and is home to snow leopards, tigers, red pandas, and other important species.

5. Bumdeling Wildlife Sanctuary

Located in the northeastern region of Bhutan, Bumdeling is particularly significant due to its protection of migratory bird species, such as the black-necked crane. The sanctuary also protects a variety of mammal species and provides vital conservation efforts for the region's wildlife.

6. Wangchuck Centennial Park

This park is situated in central Bhutan and plays an important role in conserving species like the snow leopard, blue sheep, and Himalayan tahr. It is also an essential corridor for wildlife movement and part of Bhutan's biological diversity conservation strategy.

7. Royal Manas National Park

One of the most famous parks in Bhutan, it is located in the southern foothills and shares a border with India. The park is home to rare and endangered species such as the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, elephants, and several species of primates and birds. Although it's a national park, it functions similarly to a wildlife sanctuary.
