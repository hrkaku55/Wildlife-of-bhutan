Biodiversity is crucial for Bhutan because it supports the countrys rich natural heritage and is deeply intertwined with its cultural and spiritual identity. Bhutan's diverse ecosystems, ranging from tropical forests to alpine meadows, provide vital resources such as clean water, medicinal plants, and raw materials for local communities. These ecosystems also play a key role in regulating the climate, maintaining soil health, and preventing natural disasters like floods and landslides. As a predominantly rural nation, many Bhutanese people depend directly on biodiversity for their livelihoods, making the preservation of these ecosystems a matter of both cultural and economic importance. Additionally, Bhutan has made environmental conservation a cornerstone of its national policies, particularly through its commitment to maintaining a carbon-negative status and its unique Gross National Happiness (GNH) framework. By prioritizing biodiversity conservation, Bhutan ensures the resilience of its ecosystems in the face of climate change and habitat destruction.
